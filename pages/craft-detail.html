Textile Arts

Weaving, Embroidery & Dyeing Traditions

About This Craft

Indian textile arts represent one of the oldest and most diverse craft traditions in the world. From the intricate Banarasi silk weaves to the vibrant Bandhani tie-dye of Gujarat, each region has developed unique techniques over centuries.

These crafts are not just about creating fabric; they embody cultural stories, religious symbolism, and the artistic expression of countless artisans who have dedicated their lives to perfecting these skills.

Key Techniques

  • Banarasi Weaving: Intricate silk weaving with gold and silver threads
  • Bandhani: Tie-dye technique creating vibrant patterns
  • Chikankari: Delicate white embroidery from Lucknow
  • Kalamkari: Hand-painted or block-printed textiles
  • Phulkari: Colorful floral embroidery from Punjab
