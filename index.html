The Story of Chikankari

History

Chikankari, the delicate art of white embroidery, traces its origins to the Mughal era. Legend has it that Noor Jahan, the wife of Emperor Jahangir, introduced this craft to India. For over 400 years, this ethereal art has been passed down through generations in Lucknow.

The Process

Each Chikankari piece involves 32 different stitches, meticulously hand-embroidered on fine muslin or silk. The process begins with block printing the design, followed by months of careful needlework. A single saree can take 3-6 months to complete.

Cultural Value

Chikankari is not just embroidery‚Äîit's a living heritage. It provides livelihood to over 100,000 artisans in Lucknow and surrounding regions. Each stitch carries centuries of tradition and the skilled hands of master craftspeople.
